# bpdl-rethinking
Home for BPDL's collaborative journey through Richard Mcelreath's Statistical Rethinking course.

## Context

The founding of a DataLab demands that we develop a common language with which to discuss and collaborate on statistical modelling and inference.
Working through Statistical Rethinking offers an opportunity to begin that common language on terms which lend themselves to the endeavour, and which align with broader acceptance and endorsement of Bayesian approaches to research.
Since the course begins at the beginning, assuming little in the way of prerequisite knowledge, it's also a convenient and supportive way to establish an analytical capacity floor for the DataLab team.

## Provisional Logistics

- one unit of the course per two weeks of calendar time, with the ten units of the course being completed between the beginning of March and the end of June 2023.
- expected weekly time commitment (3-5 hours):
    + 1 hour of lectures
    + 1-2 hours of reading
    + 1-2 hours of homework
    + 30 to 90 minutes of group discussion or collaborative work.

## What happens in a unit?

Each unit is planned to take two weeks.
During the first week of a unit BP rethinkers will watch the lectures and do their best to address any remaining confusion with or curiosity about the material with the unit's reading.
The first week of a unit will end with a brief discussion to highlight and work through any stumbling blocks in the material.
Over the second week, BP rethinkers will complete the homework questions for the unit.
The second week will end with a somewhat longer meeting to reflect on the unit, showcase solutions to the homework problems, and preface the next unit.
